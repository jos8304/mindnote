  • Get used to happiness.

    People are attracted to what is familiar. Develop positive emotional habits and get used to happiness.

  • Why write an emotional diary?

    I can express my honest feelings. I can identify latent emotions that I didn't even know I had.
    It gives me the opportunity to find and correct the irrational thoughts that are bothering me. I can think about whether my response to my feelings was appropriate.

  • How do I use it?

    I am writing down a situation I experienced. Write down what you felt at that time. Write down the actions or physical changes you did at that time.
    Write down your current thoughts and any plans that come to mind. It's not something to show to anyone. Write as honestly as possible.
    Don't write down vague feelings, but write down specific and clear feelings.
    In any situation other than ‘I felt bad’ [anxious, sad, resentful, depressed, embarrassed]
    I felt like it. For that reason, I developed a [grateful, happy, loving, happy] heart.

  • Example: It takes courage to lay down the weapon called 'secret'.

    Even though I said the same thing, no one listened to me. I felt so upset and resentful because I felt like I had been ignored.
    I thought it was an unreasonable treatment, but I couldn't get angry. I thought that if I got angry, it would ultimately have bad consequences for me.
    It's not the first time something like this has happened, but it seems like it happens every time. I was depressed. I stayed alone for a while.
    Next time something like this happens again, I will have to express it so that the people present know that I am in a bad mood.
    Isn’t this a necessary action to have a healthy relationship with each other?
